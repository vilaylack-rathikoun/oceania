WHY PROTECT OUR OCEANS?

The oceans are a vital element for life on Earth. Without healthy oceans, our generations will not survive.

Learn More

They allow us to breathe

Did you know that half of the oxygen we breathe comes from the oceans? Phytoplankton, a real “carbon pump”, absorbs around 30% of global CO2 emissions to transform it into oxygen. But pollution, the warming of the waters and the acidification of the oceans are gradually making this plankton disappear.

Over three billion people feed on the sea

Almost half of the world's population rely on fish as their main source of protein. Across the world, small fishing enterprises provide hundreds of millions of people with food and fixed income. The most vulnerable people depend on the oceans for their survival.

The oceans regulate the climate

The oceans have a formidable regulating effect on the temperatures of our planet. They absorb, store and transport heat from the sun in their movement, affecting the temperature and circulation of the atmosphere.

About us

Oceania is a project organized by a private organisation, which is based in Bali, Indonesia. Every year, we encourage the tourists and people from all around the world to take part in this project. Oceanina is dedicated to securing permanent, strong protection for the oceans’ most important places – for us and future generations.

The ocean is the Earth’s biggest life support system, and healthy marine ecosystems are essential for human survival and well-being. As a leader in the global movement to protect vast ocean areas, Oceanina uses the latest science to identify important marine ecosystems, advocate for their protection, and measure progress toward effective, sustainable marine protection. Our priority is to help create an urgently-needed worldwide system of Blue Parks, or recognized highly protected areas, to ensure the future diversity and abundance of marine life.

Our Mission and Vision

Building upon a strong foundation of science, partnership and field demonstration, Oceania empowers societies to responsibly and sustainably care for nature, our global biodiversity, for the well-being of humanity.

We imagine a healthy, prosperous world in which societies are forever committed to caring for and valuing nature, for the long-term benefit of people and all life on Earth.
